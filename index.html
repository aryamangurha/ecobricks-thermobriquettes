Our Flagship Product

Engineered for industrial performance

Thermobriquettes are high-density biomass fuel units produced from sugarcane bagasse, rice husk, and lignocellulosic agricultural waste — processed through advanced densification for consistent calorific output, low ash, and reliable combustion.

01

Controlled Densification Process

Moisture regulation, particle size standardization, and high-pressure compaction for uniform burn characteristics.

02

Drop-in Industrial Compatibility

Designed for boilers, furnaces, kilns, dryers, and steam generation with minimal infrastructure changes.

03

Circular Economy Sourcing

Procured from farmers and FPOs — converting waste that would otherwise be burnt in fields into premium fuel.

04

ESG & Compliance Ready

Verified CO₂ reduction reports, green certifications, and eligibility for carbon credits.

Understanding the market landscape

S

Strengths

  • Cost-efficient alternative to fossil fuels
  • Low-emission, ESG-aligned fuel profile
  • Utilizes abundant agri-residue supply
  • Modular, scalable production model
W

Weaknesses

  • Lower energy density compared to coal
  • Dependence on seasonal agri-waste supply cycles
  • Requires consistent moisture management
O

Opportunities

  • Rising environmental regulations favouring biomass
  • Increasing ESG mandates across industry
  • Government push for biomass energy adoption
  • Untapped demand across India's SME sector
T

Threats

  • Coal price fluctuations affecting cost advantage
  • Emergence of alternative clean fuels
  • Storage risks for bulk biomass inventory
  • Potential policy shifts in energy regulations
P

Political

Strong government support for biomass energy. Policy alignment with stubble burning reduction programs and national renewable energy targets.

E

Economic

Volatile fossil fuel prices create cost-advantage windows. Industrial sector actively seeking cost-stable thermal energy alternatives.

S

Social

Rising environmental awareness among industries. Positive rural impact via farmer income from agri-residue procurement.

T

Technological

Advancements in densification and briquetting technology. Continuous need for quality standardization and process innovation.

E

Environmental

Significant CO₂ and particulate emission reductions. Mitigates open-field burning of agricultural waste across India.

L

Legal

Tightening industrial emission norms (CPCB/NCAP). Potential eligibility for carbon credits and official green certifications.

Marketing Mix

The 4P + 3P framework

P

Product

High-performance biomass briquettes engineered from sugarcane bagasse. Features high densification, <2% rejection rate, and consistent moisture control for industrial reliability.

P

Price

Targeted 10–25% cost savings over coal and LPG. Competitive cost per thermal unit protects industrial margins from volatile fossil fuel prices.

P

Place

Decentralized local fuel ecosystem targeting industrial clusters in textiles, brick kilns, and food processing via local transporters and warehouse operators.

P

Promotion

Positioned as the "Clean Thermal Energy Partner" for ESG compliance. B2B outreach via pilot conversions to prove efficiency to industrial buyers.

P

People

Direct farmer and FPO partnerships for raw material sourcing. Technical teams provide expert guidance for seamless industrial boiler conversions.

P

Process

Circular waste-to-energy model: residue collection → advanced densification → standardized quality testing. Streamlined onboarding via 20+ pilot projects.

P

Physical Evidence

High-density briquette product, verified CO₂ reduction reports (40–60% lower emissions), and official ESG/green certifications as tangible proof.
